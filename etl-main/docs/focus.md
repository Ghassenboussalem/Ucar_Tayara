HACK4UCAR 2025 
Problem Statement 
University of Carthage (UCAR) · Digitalization & Institutional Intelligence 
1. Context & Problem Statement 
The University of Carthage (UCAR) oversees more than 30 affiliated institutions, each operating 
with fragmented, non-digitalized systems. This operational reality generates major inefficiencies 
at every level of governance. 
Current State — Key Findings 
● Data scattered across multiple formats (paper, Excel files, disconnected tools) 
● No centralized system for data management 
● Slow and inefficient decision-making processes 
● Complex and unreliable performance monitoring across institutions 
Observed Impacts 
● Poor visibility over operational and academic data 
● Redundant work across teams and institutions 
● Limited coordination between affiliated institutions 
● Inability to monitor overall performance in real time 
2. Your Mission 
Design and develop AI-powered solutions to help UCAR: 
● Centralize and structure operational, academic, and financial data 
● Automate repetitive administrative processes 
● Enable smarter, data-driven strategic decision-making 
● Improve communication and coordination across institutions 
�
�  Target Vision 
Build an intelligent university management platform enabling real-time centralization of 
operational, academic, financial, and environmental data, in service of strategic decision
making driven by key performance indicators (KPIs). 
3. Expected Deliverables 
Participants must deliver: 
 
● A working prototype 
◦ Web and/or mobile application, scalable to 30+ institutions 
● A clear and demonstrated AI integration 
◦ AI must be at the core of the solution, not just a surface-level UI element 
● A short pitch (5 to 7 minutes) 
◦ Explaining impact, feasibility, and scalability 
 
4. Judging Criteria 
 
Criterion Description 
Impact Real usefulness for UCAR, addressing genuine institutional needs 
Innovation Quality and depth of AI integration in the solution 
Usability Ease of use for non-technical staff 
Scalability Ability to handle 30+ institutions without degradation 
Feasibility Realism of deployment in a Tunisian university context
